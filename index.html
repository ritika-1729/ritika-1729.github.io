About Me

Hello world! I'm Ritika, and I'm currently a master's student at Virginia Tech studying computer science. My research focuses on the intersection of explainable AI, AI safety, and their applications in generative models.

Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of natural language processing, machine learning, and interpretable AI. I am particularly focused on:

  • Developing interpretable neural networks for NLP tasks
  • Reducing computational requirements for large language models
  • Fairness and bias detection in language models
  • Novel approaches to few-shot learning in NLP
  • Efficient knowledge representation in language understanding

Publications

Efficient Attention Mechanisms for Low-Resource NLP

Johnson, A., Wilson, S., & Patel, R.

International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML)

2024

This paper introduces a novel attention mechanism that reduces computational requirements while maintaining performance on key NLP benchmarks. We demonstrate significant efficiency gains on low-resource languages.

Interpretable Neural Models for Text Classification

Johnson, A., & Wilson, S.

Proceedings of the Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL)

2023

We propose a new architecture for text classification that provides human-interpretable explanations for its decisions. Our approach achieves state-of-the-art performance while offering insights into model decision-making.

Detecting and Mitigating Bias in Word Embeddings

Patel, R., Johnson, A., & Lee, J.

Ethics in Natural Language Processing Workshop

2022

This work presents a framework for identifying and reducing unwanted biases in word embeddings without compromising their utility for downstream tasks.
